Car Insurance Claims - Tableau Dashboard

Data Visualization & Business Intelligence | UT Austin PG Data Science

Business Context

An insurance company wants to understand patterns in car insurance claims to optimize pricing strategies, identify high-risk segments, and improve fraud detection. The leadership team needs an interactive dashboard to explore claims data and derive actionable insights.

Objective

Create an interactive Tableau storyboard to present insights to Senior Management:

  • Analyze claims distribution by demographics and vehicle characteristics
  • Identify patterns in claim amounts and frequencies
  • Discover risk factors for high-value claims
  • Provide recommendations for risk-based pricing

Dashboard Components

Age Distribution

Claims distribution across age groups with average claim amounts and frequency analysis

Vehicle Analysis

Claims by vehicle type, age, and value - identifying high-risk vehicle categories

Geographic Trends

Regional claims patterns and urban vs. rural analysis

Temporal Patterns

Seasonal trends, monthly variations, and claim timing analysis

Risk Profiling

High-risk segment identification and fraud indicators

Financial Impact

Claim amounts, loss ratios, and profitability analysis

Key Insights

Young drivers (18-25): 40% higher claim frequency than average

Sports cars: 2.3x average claim amount compared to sedans

Urban areas: Higher frequency but lower severity claims

Winter months: 25% increase in collision claims

Recommendations to Management

  • Risk-based pricing: Increase premiums for high-risk segments (young drivers, sports cars)
  • Seasonal adjustments: Offer winter driving discounts for customers with safety features
  • Fraud detection: Flag claims matching suspicious patterns for investigation
  • Customer segmentation: Develop targeted products for low-risk demographics
  • Geographic strategy: Adjust underwriting criteria by region
