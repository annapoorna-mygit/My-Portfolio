INN Hotels - Booking Cancellation Prediction

Supervised Machine Learning | UT Austin PG Data Science

Business Context

INN Hotels Group operates a chain of hotels across Portugal. They face a significant challenge with booking cancellations, which directly impacts revenue and resource planning. A high cancellation rate leads to loss of revenue, suboptimal resource allocation, and difficulty in demand forecasting.

Objective

Build a predictive model to identify bookings likely to be canceled, enabling:

  • Targeted retention strategies for high-risk bookings
  • Optimized overbooking policies
  • Improved revenue management
  • Enhanced customer experience through proactive engagement

Technical Approach

1. Exploratory Data Analysis:

  • Analyzed 36,275 booking records with 17 features
  • Identified patterns in lead times, deposit types, and booking channels
  • Discovered seasonal trends and customer segment behaviors

2. Feature Engineering:

  • Created derived features: total guests, stay duration, booking changes
  • Encoded categorical variables using one-hot encoding
  • Applied feature scaling for distance-based algorithms

3. Model Development:

  • Implemented Logistic Regression, Decision Trees, Random Forest
  • Handled class imbalance using SMOTE
  • Performed hyperparameter tuning with GridSearchCV

Key Findings

73% of cancellations come from bookings with lead time > 100 days

2.5x higher cancellation rate for bookings without deposit

45% of online travel agent bookings get canceled

Model Performance

Model Accuracy Precision Recall F1-Score
Logistic Regression 79% 76% 72% 74%
Decision Tree 82% 80% 78% 79%
Random Forest 86% 84% 82% 83%

Business Recommendations

  • Early intervention: Contact high-risk bookings 2 weeks before arrival
  • Deposit policy: Require deposits for bookings with lead time > 60 days
  • Channel management: Implement stricter policies for OTA bookings
  • Dynamic pricing: Offer incentives for non-refundable bookings
