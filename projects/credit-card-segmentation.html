Credit Card Customer Segmentation

Unsupervised Machine Learning | UT Austin PG Data Science

Business Context

AllLife Bank wants to focus on its credit card customer base to increase market penetration. The marketing team needs to understand different customer segments to design targeted campaigns and personalized product offerings instead of one-size-fits-all approaches.

Objective

Identify distinct customer segments using unsupervised learning to enable:

  • Targeted marketing campaigns for each segment
  • Segment-specific credit card products and features
  • Optimized customer acquisition costs
  • Improved customer retention strategies

Technical Approach

1. Data Preparation:

  • Analyzed 8,950 credit card customers with 18 behavioral features
  • Features: balance, purchases, cash advances, credit limit, payments, tenure
  • Standardized features using StandardScaler

2. Dimensionality Reduction:

  • Applied PCA to reduce 18 features while preserving 85% variance
  • Visualized clusters in 2D space for interpretation

3. Clustering:

  • Used Elbow Method and Silhouette Score to determine optimal K=4
  • Applied K-Means clustering algorithm
  • Validated with Hierarchical Clustering (Agglomerative)
